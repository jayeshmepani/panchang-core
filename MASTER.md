# 1. Core Panchang (The Five Limbs of Time)

These five elements are calculated daily based on the positions of the Sun and the Moon.

*   **Vara (Weekday):** The seven days of the week, calculated from local sunrise to the next sunrise:
    > *Ravivara (Sunday), Somavara (Monday), Mangalavara (Tuesday), Budhavara (Wednesday), Guruvara (Thursday), Shukravara (Friday), Shanivara (Saturday)*
*   **Tithi (Lunar Day):** The thirty phases of the moon, divided into two fortnights (fifteen in the waxing phase and fifteen in the waning phase):
    > *Pratipada (Prathama), Dwitiya, Tritiya, Chaturthi, Panchami, Shashti, Saptami, Ashtami, Navami, Dashami, Ekadashi, Dwadashi, Trayodashi, Chaturdashi, Purnima (Full Moon) / Amavasya (New Moon)*
*   **Nakshatra (Lunar Mansion):** The segment of the ecliptic the Moon resides in. There are 27 primary Nakshatras:
    > *Ashwini, Bharani, Krittika, Rohini, Mrigashira, Ardra, Punarvasu, Pushya, Ashlesha, Magha, Purva Phalguni, Uttara Phalguni, Hasta, Chitra, Swati, Vishakha, Anuradha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Purva Ashadha, Uttara Ashadha, Shravana, Dhanishta, Shatabhisha, Purva Bhadrapada, Uttara Bhadrapada, Revati*
*   **Karana (Half of a Tithi):** There are 11 Karanas, consisting of 7 repeating/mobile (Chara) Karanas and 4 fixed/immobile (Sthira) Karanas:
    *   *Repeating (Chara) Karanas:* *Bava, Balava, Kaulava, Taitila, Gara, Vanija, Vishti (Bhadra)*
    *   *Fixed (Sthira) Karanas:* *Shakuni, Chatushpada, Naga, Kintughna*
*   **Yoga (Luni-Solar Relationship):** There are 27 major Yogas:
    > *Vishkumbha, Priti, Ayushman, Saubhagya, Shobhana, Atiganda, Sukarma, Dhriti, Shula, Ganda, Vriddhi, Dhruva, Vyaghata, Harshana, Vajra, Siddhi, Vyatipata, Variyana, Parigha, Shiva, Siddha, Sadhya, Shubha, Shukla, Brahma, Indra, Vaidhriti*

# 2. Astronomical & Solar/Lunar Parameters

*   **Paksha:** The two lunar fortnights of a month:
    *   *Shukla Paksha:* The waxing fortnight (from New Moon to Full Moon).
    *   *Krishna Paksha:* The waning fortnight (from Full Moon to New Moon).
*   **Maas (Lunar Months):** The twelve canonical lunar months of the calendar year:
    > *Chaitra, Vaishakha, Jyeshtha, Ashadha, Shravana, Bhadrapada, Ashvina, Kartika, Margashirsha, Pausha, Magha, Phalguna*
    *   *Adhika Maas:* The intercalary/extra month added approximately every 32.5 months to align the lunar year with the solar year.
    *   *Kshaya Maas:* The rare skipped/lost month when two solar transits occur within a single lunar month.
*   **Sankranti:** The moment the Sun enters a new zodiac sign. There are twelve seasonal Sankrantis:
    > *Mesha Sankranti, Vrishabha Sankranti, Mithuna Sankranti, Karka Sankranti, Simha Sankranti, Kanya Sankranti, Tula Sankranti, Vrischika Sankranti, Dhanu Sankranti, Makara Sankranti, Kumbha Sankranti, Meena Sankranti*
*   **Ishtkaal:** The time elapsed from local sunrise to a specific target event, measured traditionally in Ghadis (24 minutes) and Palas (24 seconds).
*   **Sun & Moon Dynamics:**
    *   *Sunrise & Sunset:* The physical appearance and disappearance of the Sun's upper limb at the horizon.
    *   *Moonrise & Moonset:* The daily appearance and disappearance of the Moon.
    *   *Sunsign & Moonsign:* The specific constellation (Rashi) the Sun and Moon occupy:
        > *Aries (Mesha), Taurus (Vrishabha), Gemini (Mithuna), Cancer (Karka), Leo (Simha), Virgo (Kanya), Libra (Tula), Scorpio (Vrischika), Sagittarius (Dhanu), Capricorn (Makara), Aquarius (Kumbha), Pisces (Meena)*
*   **Day & Night Measures:**
    *   *Dinamana:* Total duration of daytime (interval from sunrise to sunset).
    *   *Ratrimana:* Total duration of nighttime (interval from sunset to the next sunrise).
*   **Twilight Phases (Dawn & Dusk):**
    *   *Civil Twilight:* Calculated when the Sun is 6 degrees below the horizon.
    *   *Nautical Twilight:* Calculated when the Sun is 12 degrees below the horizon.
    *   *Astronomical Twilight:* Calculated when the Sun is 18 degrees below the horizon.

# 3. Calendar Eras & Chronology (Samvat)

*   **Ayana:** The two half-year paths of the Sun:
    *   *Uttarayana:* The northern course of the Sun (from winter solstice to summer solstice).
    *   *Dakshinayana:* The southern course of the Sun (from summer solstice to winter solstice).
*   **Ritu:** The six traditional seasons:
    *   *Vasanta:* Spring (Chaitra to Vaishakha)
    *   *Grishma:* Summer (Jyeshtha to Ashadha)
    *   *Varsha:* Monsoon (Shravana to Bhadrapada)
    *   *Sharad:* Autumn (Ashvina to Kartika)
    *   *Hemant:* Pre-winter (Margashirsha to Pausha)
    *   *Shishir:* Winter (Magha to Phalguna)
*   **Regional Month Systems:**
    *   *Month (Amanta):* Month-tracking system where the month ends on the New Moon (Amavasya); common in South and West India.
    *   *Month (Purnimanta):* Month-tracking system where the month ends on the Full Moon (Purnima); common in North India.
*   **Calendar Eras:**
    *   *Vikram Samvat:* Solar/lunar era starting in 57 BCE.
    *   *Gujarati Samvat:* Variant of Vikram Samvat starting from the day after Diwali (Kartika Shukla Pratipada).
    *   *Saka Samvat:* National civil calendar era starting in 78 CE.
    *   *Kali Samvat:* Chronological era starting from the departure of Lord Krishna (3102 BCE).
*   **60-Year Jovian Cycle (Samvatsara):**
    *   *Samvatsara (South):* **Parabhava** (The 40th year of the continuous sequence).
    *   *Samvatsara (North):* **Siddharthi** (The 53rd year, calculated using Jupiter's actual transits).

### Note on the 13-Year Samvatsara Gap
The difference in Samvatsara names between North and South India is a result of different mathematical calculation systems rather than differences in month-tracking (Amanta or Purnimanta):
1.  **Southern Calculation:** The 60-year cycle functions as a continuous sequence. The year name progresses by exactly one step with each solar/lunar year. Under this system, the current year is **Parabhava** (#40).
2.  **Northern Calculation:** The cycle is adjusted to align with the actual transit speed of Jupiter (*Brihaspati*). Since a true Jovian transit takes approximately 361 days (slightly shorter than a solar year), Northern astronomy drops (skips) one Samvatsara name about every 85 years to keep pace with the physical planet. 

Over centuries, this practice of skipping years in the North while maintaining continuous tracking in the South has created a 13-year divergence, resulting in the North currently observing **Siddharthi** (#53).

# 4. Special Astrological Yogas & Sensitivities

### Major Auspicious & Inauspicious Yogas
*   **Sarvartha Siddhi Yoga:** Auspicious window formed by favorable weekday and Nakshatra alignments, suitable for beginning new ventures.
*   **Amrit Siddhi Yoga:** Special highly auspicious configuration that brings long-term positive outcomes.
*   **Ravi Yoga:** Protective solar configuration that neutralizes other minor day flaws.
*   **Ravi Pushya Yoga:** Occurs when the auspicious Pushya Nakshatra falls on a Sunday.
*   **Guru Pushya Yoga:** Occurs when the auspicious Pushya Nakshatra falls on a Thursday.
*   **Dwipushkar Yoga:** Formed by specific combinations of weekday, Tithi, and Nakshatra, double-multiplying the impact of any action performed.
*   **Tripushkar Yoga:** Formed by specific alignments that triple-multiply the consequences of deeds.

### Regional & Nakshatra-Based Sensitivities
*   **Ganda Mula:** Astrological vulnerability occurring when the Moon transits the transitional zones between water signs and fire signs, covering six Nakshatras:
    > *Ashwini, Ashlesha, Magha, Jyeshtha, Mula, Revati*
*   **Vinchhudo:** The transit of the Moon through Scorpio (Vrishchika Rashi), considered sensitive for initiation.
*   **Aadal Yoga & Vidaal Yoga:** Daily energetic conditions based on weekday and Nakshatra pairings.
*   **Jwalamukhi Yoga:** An adverse daily combination of specific Tithis and Nakshatras.

### Anandadi Yoga System
A 28-Nakshatra daily calculation framework (utilizing the intercalary Nakshatra, Abhijit) that yields daily energetic trends:
> *Ananda, Kaladanda, Dhumra, Dhata/Prajapati, Saumya, Dhvanksha, Dhvaja, Srivatsa, Vajra, Mudgara, Chhatra, Mitra, Manasa, Padma, Lumbaka, Utpata, Mrityu, Kana, Siddhi, Shubha, Amrita, Mushala, Gada, Matanga, Rakshasa, Chara, Sthira, Vardhamana*

### Amritadi Yoga System
A 27-Nakshatra classification system based on weekday-Nakshatra combinations, yielding four primary qualitative daily results:
1.  **Siddha** (Auspicious)
2.  **Amrita** (Highly Auspicious)
3.  **Marana** (Inauspicious/Adverse)
4.  **Prabalarishta** (Inauspicious/Obstacles)

### Specific Planetary Configurations
*   **Panchak:** The sensitive five-day transit period of the Moon through the last segment of the zodiac, spanning five Nakshatras:
    > *Dhanishta (latter half), Shatabhisha, Purva Bhadrapada, Uttara Bhadrapada, Revati*
    The package calculates Panchak using the Moon longitude range from **Dhanishta pada 3 through Revati** (300°–360°) and locks the subtype to the weekday at the true Panchak entry moment. The current running weekday inside the active Panchak span is exposed separately.

    **Panchak subtype by entry weekday:**
    *   **Sunday:** *Roga Panchaka* (illness/disease sensitivity)
    *   **Monday:** *Raja Panchaka* (authority/government/status sensitivity)
    *   **Tuesday:** *Agni Panchaka* (fire/heat/accident sensitivity)
    *   **Friday:** *Chora Panchaka* (theft/loss/security sensitivity)
    *   **Saturday:** *Mrityu Panchaka* (danger/mortality sensitivity)
    *   **Wednesday / Thursday:** *Shubha Panchaka* / Panchak without the five named weekday dosha subtypes
*   **Maitreya Yoga:** Formed by the precise alignment of specific weekdays, Nakshatras, and rising signs (Lagnas).
*   **Gajachchhaya Yoga:** Highly specific planetary alignments observed during Pitru Paksha, categorized by three variants:
    *   *Variant 1:* Trayodashi Tithi with the Sun in Hasta Nakshatra and the Moon in Magha Nakshatra.
    *   *Variant 2:* Amavasya Tithi with the Sun in Hasta Nakshatra and the Moon in Magha Nakshatra.
    *   *Variant 3:* Bhadrapada Krishna Trayodashi during Pitru Paksha with the Sun in Hasta Nakshatra and the Moon in Magha Nakshatra.

# 5. Astrological Directions, Residences, and Doshas

### Shoola & Vaasa (Directions and Residences of Energies)
*   **Disha Shool:** Daily inauspicious travel directions based on the weekday:
    *   *Sunday & Friday:* West
    *   *Monday & Saturday:* East
    *   *Tuesday & Wednesday:* North
    *   *Thursday:* South
*   **Nakshatra Shool:** Adverse travel directions determined by the current active Nakshatra.
*   **Rahu Vaasa:** The specific directional sector occupied by Rahu on a given day.
*   **Chandra Vaasa:** The direction of the Moon's presence, considered favorable to face during travel or transactions.
*   **Shiva Vaasa:** The symbolic location of Lord Shiva (e.g., Kailash, Gauri-Sannidhya, Sabha), analyzed to check the suitability of performing Rudrabhishek.
*   **Agni Vaasa:** The presence of the Fire element (either on Earth or in the Heavens), calculated to verify the suitability of performing Homa/Yajna.
*   **Yogini Vaasa:** The daily directional residence of the Yogini deity based on the current Tithi.

### Panchaka Rahita (Muhurta Purification)
Calculations applied to determine the purity of an hour for major life events. The six potential flaws (Doshas) are:
*   **Mrityu** (Danger/Hardship)
*   **Dagdha** (Loss/Burnt outcomes)
*   **Visha** (Obstacles/Poisonous results)
*   **Hutashana** (Fire hazards)
*   **Krakacha** (Sharp conflict/Instruments)
*   **Samvarta** (Ruins/Destruction)

This electional remainder check is separate from the Moon-in-Panchak weekday subtype system. In practice, it is used as a muhurta-screening layer rather than as a general daily Panchak transit label.

### Vara Tithi Doshas
Specific daily flaws arising when a weekday and a particular Tithi collide:
*   *Mrityu, Dagdha, Visha, Krakacha, Samvarta*

### Daily Screening and Observance Layers
The package also includes several daily interpretive layers for non-natal panchang screening:
*   **Nitya Yoga Observations:** Flags special concerns in Yogas such as Vyatipata and Vaidhriti.
*   **Vara-Tithi Doshas:** Checks flaws caused by weekday and Tithi combinations.
*   **Tithi Observance Analysis:** Distinguishes Shuddha, Viddha, Kshaya, and Vriddhi-style sunrise observance states.
*   **Vrata Parana:** Gives parana guidance for supported fasting families.
*   **Yatra Screening:** Evaluates travel direction concerns and blocked directions.
*   **Day and Night Measures:** Presents Dinamana and Ratrimana in modern duration units as well as Ghati and Pala.
*   **Nakshatra Tyajya:** Shows the Varjyam-like avoided portion of the active Nakshatra.
*   **Dharma Sindhu Style Notes:** Preserves observance and Punya-Kala style guidance where the package has a supported rule family.

### Bhadra, Parana, and Nirnay Refinements
The package includes deeper observance-resolution helpers beyond simple Tithi labels:
*   **Bhadra / Vishti Handling:** Supports Bhadra period handling, including Bhadra Mukha, Bhadra Madhya, and Bhadra Puchha style preference or rejection rules where festival decisions need them.
*   **Ekadashi Parana:** Ekadashi handling includes Harivasara, Dwadashi-parana windows, restricted/allowed parana intervals, and emergency symbolic-water parana notes where the supported tradition profile requires it.

# 6. Time Subdivisions & Daily Muhurtas

### Standard Hourly Divisions
*   **Lagna (Ascendant):** The rising sign on the eastern horizon. There are twelve distinct Lagnas throughout a 24-hour day:
    > *Mesha (Aries), Vrishabha (Taurus), Mithuna (Gemini), Karka (Cancer), Simha (Leo), Kanya (Virgo), Tula (Libra), Vrischika (Scorpio), Dhanu (Sagittarius), Makara (Capricorn), Kumbha (Aquarius), Meena (Pisces)*
*   **Hora:** Hourly intervals (approx. 60 minutes) ruled sequentially by seven planetary lords:
    > *Surya (Sun), Chandra (Moon), Mangala (Mars), Budha (Mercury), Guru (Jupiter), Shukra (Venus), Shani (Saturn)*

### Choghadiya (16 Divisions: 8 Day and 8 Night)
Each day-part and night-part is divided into 8 equal portions, classified into seven distinct spiritual qualities:
*   *Beneficial:* **Amrit** (Nectar), **Shubh** (Auspicious), **Labh** (Gain), **Chal** (Neutral/Fluctuating)
*   *Inauspicious:* **Udveg** (Anxiety), **Rog** (Disease), **Kaal** (Loss/Time)

### Gowri Panchangam
A southern system of time division similar to Choghadiya, categorized into seven daily qualities:
*   *Beneficial:* **Amirdha** (Nectar), **Dhanam** (Wealth), **Sugam** (Comfort/Happiness), **Uthi** (Rise/Progress)
*   *Inauspicious:* **Rogam** (Illness), **Soram** (Theft/Loss), **Visham** (Obstacles)

### Kala Velas (Upagraha and Sensitive Intervals)
Daily sub-periods representing planetary shadow zones:
*   *Kala, Mrityu, Ardhaprahara, Yamaghantaka, Gulika*

### Classical Karmakala & Special Windows
Specific temporal windows allocated for daily rituals, duties, and astrological consideration:

*   **Rahu Kaal:** Daily inauspicious period ruled by Rahu.
*   **Gulika Kaal:** Daily period ruled by Gulika.
*   **Yamaganda:** Daily inauspicious period ruled by Ketu.
*   **Abhijit Muhurta:** Highly auspicious mid-day window (around the 8th Muhurta of the day).
*   **Brahma Muhurta:** Time of early morning spiritual practice (approx. 1.5 hours before sunrise).
*   **Dur Muhurta / Durmuhurta:** Certain muhūrtas that are specially considered inauspicious on particular weekdays, according to the Nārada Saṃhitā. These are to be avoided for performing auspicious activities (śubha-karma). They are distinct from the general auspicious or inauspicious nature of the muhūrtas listed above.

    The traditional rule is stated in this verse:

    > अर्यमा राक्षसब्राह्मौ पित्र्याग्नेयौ तथाभिजित् । राक्षसापो ब्रह्मपित्र्यौ भौजंगेशाविनादिषु ॥ वारेषु वर्जनीयास्ते मुहूर्ताः शुभकर्मसु ॥

    In practice, the muhūrtas to be avoided on each weekday are:

    - Sunday: Aryamā (the 14th muhūrta)
    - Monday: Nirṛti (Rākṣasa) and Brahmā (the 12th and 9th)
    - Tuesday: Pitṛ and Agni (Vahni) (the 4th and the 22nd in the full sequence)
    - Wednesday: Vidhātṛ (also called Abhijit or Vidhi) (the 8th muhūrta)
    - Thursday: Nirṛti and Udaka (Jala) (the 12th and 6th)
    - Friday: Brahmā and Pitṛ (the 9th and 4th)
    - Saturday: Ahi (Sarpa) and Īśa (Śiva) (the 2nd and 16th)

    Note that these weekday-specific Durmuhūrtas form a separate layer from the fixed qualities of the thirty muhūrtas. The numbering refers to the traditional position within the day or night sequence (for example, the 14th muhūrta of the day on Sunday).
*   **Nishita Muhurta:** Midnight auspicious window, especially for spiritual practices.
*   **Vijaya Muhurta:** Late afternoon auspicious period for success and victory.
*   **Godhuli Muhurta:** Period around sunset.
*   **Pradosha Kaal:** Twilight period dedicated to Lord Shiva.
*   **Varjyam / Nakshatra Tyajya:** Inauspicious portion of a Nakshatra's duration.
*   **Amrita Kaal:** Auspicious portion of a Nakshatra's duration.

The package also covers full-day tables for Rahu Kaal, Gulika, Yamaganda, Hora, Choghadiya, the 30 Muhurtas, Prahara, Dur Muhurta, Lagna, and Karmakala windows.

# 8. Time Segments & Ritual Divisions

### Praharas (8-Fold Division of the Day and Night)
Four daytime Praharas and four nighttime Praharas, with their duration changing according to the actual length of daylight and night:
*   **Daytime Praharas:** *Pratah Prahara, Sangava Prahara, Madhyahna Prahara, Aparahna Prahara*
*   **Nighttime Praharas:** *Pradosha Prahara, Nishitha Prahara, Triyama Prahara, Usha Prahara*

### Fivefold Day Divisions
*   **Pratah** (Early Morning - first 1/5th of the daytime)
*   **Sangava** (Forenoon - second 1/5th of the daytime)
*   **Madhyahna** (Midday - third 1/5th of the daytime)
*   **Aparahna** (Afternoon - fourth 1/5th of the daytime)
*   **Sayahna** (Late Afternoon/Evening - final 1/5th of the daytime)

### Sandhya (Three Daily Transitions)
*   **Pratah Sandhya** (Fixed-ghati ritual window around local sunrise)
*   **Madhyahna Sandhya** (Fixed-ghati ritual window around local solar noon)
*   **Sayam Sandhya** (Fixed-ghati ritual window around local sunset)

### Karmakala Ritual Divisions
Specific divisions used to schedule actions based on religious injunctions:
*   *Arunodaya* (Pre-dawn/before sunrise)
*   *Pratah Kal* (Morning)
*   *Sangava* (Forenoon)
*   *Abhijit* (Midday)
*   *Aparahna* (Afternoon)
*   *Vijaya Kaal* (Inherent success hour)
*   *Sayankala* (Final one-fifth of the actual daytime)
*   *Nishitha* (Midnight)
*   *Pradosha* (Six fixed ghatis after local sunset)

# 10. Astronomical & Ritual Eclipse Coverage

Eclipse evaluations combine physical astronomy with scriptural standards. Rather than employing simplified checklists, eclipse systems track complete local, global, and visibility data to determine whether an eclipse has practical, ritual, or spiritual consequences.

### Core Eclipse Parameters
*   **Eclipse Classifications:** Distinguishes between Solar and Lunar events.
*   **Global vs. Local Classification:** Tracks how the eclipse behaves globally versus how it is perceived at specific local coordinates. For example, an eclipse cataloged globally as "Total" might only present as "Partial" locally.
*   **Maximum Point Data:** Evaluates the exact date and time of the maximum eclipse phase alongside its Julian Day equivalent.
*   **Magnitude and Obscuration Metrics:**
    *   *Eclipse Magnitude:* The fraction of the angular diameter of the occulted body.
    *   *Local Obscuration:* The actual fraction of the sun or moon's disk area covered by the shadow.
    *   *Ritual Minimum Magnitude:* The threshold under which an eclipse is considered spiritually negligible (typically 0.0833, or 1/12th of the disk).

### Visibility Standards & Ritual Impurity (Sutak)
Sutak and daily temple observances are based strictly on local, physical visibility rather than global occurrence.

*   **Local Visibility Analysis:** Verifies if the eclipse is visible to the unaided eye at the specified coordinates, filtering out events that are solely astronomically visible (e.g., penumbral lunar eclipses or minor partial eclipses occurring below the horizon).
*   **Ritual Visibility Thresholds:** Local ritual visibility can reject penumbral-only lunar cases, telescope-only cases, below-horizon events, and short local visibility cases that do not meet the configured minimum visible duration.
*   **Sutak Applicability:** Determines if the purification rules apply based on local visibility.
*   **Sutak Time Windows:** Calculates the precise start and end times for the period of physical restraint.
    *   *Solar Eclipse Sutak:* Commences 4 Praharas (approx. 12 hours) before the eclipse begins.
    *   *Lunar Eclipse Sutak:* Commences 3 Praharas (approx. 9 hours) before the eclipse begins.
*   **Relaxed Sutak Windows:** Standard modifications applied for children, the elderly, and those experiencing illness.

### Ritual Boundaries & Scriptural Instructions
*   **Ritual Boundary Assessment:** Identifies specific scriptural situations, such as:
    *   *Grastodaya / Grast Uday:* An eclipse that begins before sunrise, meaning the sun rises already eclipsed.
    *   *Grastasta / Grast Ast:* An eclipse that continues past sunset, meaning the sun sets while still eclipsed.
    *   *Ordinary Visible Eclipse:* A standard visible eclipse occurring entirely while the celestial body is above the horizon.
*   **Post-Eclipse Rituals:**
    *   *Snana (Bathing):* Determines if purification baths are required immediately following fourth contact (eclipse end).
    *   *Food Rules:* Provides timing for when fresh food must be prepared, as stored cooked food is conventionally discarded after visible eclipses.
