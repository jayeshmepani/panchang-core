# Comprehensive Catalog of Ancient Indian Occult Texts (Gupta-Vidyā)

**Classification:** Strictly Bifurcated into **Sage-Attributed** (Arsha) and **Non-Sage-Attributed** (Historical/Classical).

---

## Preamble: The Two Streams of Knowledge

1. **Sage-Attributed (Arsha):** Texts traditionally believed to be revealed to or authored by Vedic Ṛṣis (sages), Devarṣis (divine sages), or Avatars. These form the _Śruti_ (heard/revealed) or authoritative _Smṛti_ (remembered) foundation. Their authorship is often prehistoric or legendary.
2. **Non-Sage-Attributed (Pauruṣeya/Historical):** Texts written by historical scholars, medieval court astronomers, compilers, and commentators. These represent the scientific and systematic evolution of the knowledge, often datable (e.g., 5th Century CE onwards).

---

### Critical Scholarly Context
*While the "Sage-Attributed" classification reflects the traditional view (parampara), historical-critical scholarship often views these texts as:*
*   **Pseudepigraphic:** Composed by later authors (medieval or early modern) and attributed to ancient sages to gain authority.
*   **Composite Layers:** Collections of verses from different centuries (e.g., *Sūrya Siddhānta*, *BPHS*) rather than single-author revelations.
*   **Modern Reconstructions:** Some "ancient" texts in circulation (e.g., *Rāvaṇa Saṁhitā*, commercial *Nāḍī*) are widely considered by scholars to be modern compilations or fabrications.
*   *Scholarly/Critical notes have been added below to texts with significant authorship controversies.*

#### E. Ganita, Anka & Yantra (Classical Numerical Occultism)

_While modern "Anka-Jyotisha" often borrows from Western numerology, the classical Indian tradition contains deep "Numerical Occultism" embedded in Ganita (Mathematics) and Tantra (Yantra-Shastra)._

- **Gaṇita Kaumudī (Chapter: Bhadragaṇita)** — **Nārāyaṇa Paṇḍita** (1356 CE). The definitive classical treatise on **Magic Squares (Anka Yantra)**. It details the construction of "Bhadra-Ganita" (Auspicious Mathematics) used for ritual yantras and occult purposes.
- **Kakṣapuṭa Tantra** — Attributed to **Nāgārjuna**. Contains earliest references to using **Magic Squares (Ankais)** for magical/occult outcomes (Shatkarmas).
- **Siddhayoga** — (c. 10th Century). Prescribes specific **Anka Yantras** (numerical magic squares) for medical and magical protection (e.g., for childbirth).
- **Śiva Svarodaya** — (Also listed under Svara). Uses **"Breath Numbers"** (counts of exhalation length) for prediction and life-span analysis, a form of intrinsic bio-numerology.
- **Kaṭapayādi System** — The classical alphanumeric encoding system used in **Mantra-Shastra** and **Musicology** to derive numerical values from sounds/syllables, forming the basis of mathematical mysticism in India.
