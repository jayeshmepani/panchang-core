Here's the big picture from the research:

**The two public Navratris** — Chaitra (spring) and Sharad (autumn) — follow the identical nine-night sequence of the **Navdurgas**: Shailputri → Brahmacharini → Chandraghanta → Kushmanda → Skandamata → Katyayani → Kalaratri → Mahagauri → Siddhidatri. These nine forms represent the nine life stages of Goddess Parvati, as described in the Devikavaca section of the Devi Purana. During Navratri, Hindus worship the nine forms of Maa Durga, each representing a different aspect of the divine feminine. Sharad Navratri also follows a three-phase arc: days 1–3 honour Durga (power), days 4–6 honour Lakshmi (wealth), and days 7–9 honour Saraswati (wisdom).

**The two Gupt (secret) Navratris** — Ashadha (monsoon) and Magha (winter) — follow a completely different, tantric system. According to the Devi Bhagwat, just as the nine forms of the Goddess are worshipped during Chaitra and Sharad Navratri, during Gupt Navratri the worship of the Dus Mahavidya Goddesses is considered highly meritorious. The term "Gupt Navratri" means "the secret nine nights", dedicated to the worship of the Ten Mahavidyas — the powerful forms of Goddess Durga. These ten are: Kali, Tara, Tripura Sundari, Bhuvaneshwari, Bhairavi, Chinnamasta, Dhumavati, Bagalamukhi, Matangi, and Kamala.

Ashadha has a further layer unique to it: each night of Ashadha Gupt Navratri is also associated with a powerful form of Goddess Varahi, collectively known as the Nava Varahi. These nine boar-headed Varahi forms (Swapna Varahi, Maha Varahi, etc.) make Ashadha the richest of all four Navratris in terms of dedicated Devis per ratri.

# All four Navratris and their dedicated Devis for each ratri

## Overview

> According to the Devi Bhagavatam, all four Navratris have dedicated Devis for each ratri — but the system differs: **Chaitra & Sharad** worship the 9 Navdurgas (public), while **Ashadha & Magha** (Gupt Navratris) worship the 10 Dasha Mahavidyas in a secret/tantric tradition.

### 🌸 Chaitra Navratri
**Chaitra month (March–April) · Spring · Shukla Paksha**  
`Public` `2nd most celebrated`

9 Navdurgas worshipped each day. Ends on Ram Navami. Also called Vasanta or Rama Navratri.

### 🌿 Ashadha Gupt Navratri
**Ashadha month (June–July) · Monsoon · Shukla Paksha**  
`Gupt / Secret`

Dasha Mahavidyas + Varahi (9 Nava Varahi forms) worshipped. Primarily for Tantrikas & Sadhaks. Also called Varahi, Gayatri, or Shakambhari Navratri.

### 🍂 Sharad Navratri (Maha Navratri)
**Ashwin month (Sep–Oct) · Autumn · Shukla Paksha**  
`Public` `Most celebrated`

9 Navdurgas worshipped. Culminates in Durga Puja & Vijayadashami (Dussehra). Garba & Dandiya in Gujarat. Linked to Mahishasura Mardini legend.

### ❄️ Magha Gupt Navratri
**Magha month (Jan–Feb) · Winter · Shukla Paksha**  
`Gupt / Secret`

Dasha Mahavidyas worshipped. 5th day is Vasant Panchami (Saraswati). Also called Shishir Navratri. Primarily observed by spiritual seekers & Tantrikas.

## Chaitra Navratri — 9 Navdurgas

*Same Navdurga sequence as Sharad · Both are public / mainstream celebrations*

> Each ratri (night) is dedicated to one of the nine forms of Maa Durga, called **Navdurga**. These represent the nine life phases of Goddess Parvati. The same nine are worshipped in both Chaitra and Sharad Navratri.

### The Nine Forms

| Ratri | Tithi | Devi | Meaning |
|-------|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | Pratipada | **Maa Shailputri** | Daughter of the mountains. Combined power of Brahma, Vishnu & Shiva. Rides Nandi. Rules Moon. |
| 2 | Dwitiya | **Maa Brahmacharini** | The celibate ascetic. Symbol of penance, devotion & determination. Walked barefoot for millennia. |
| 3 | Tritiya | **Maa Chandraghanta** | Half-moon bell on forehead. Fierce warrior goddess. Symbol of bravery & grace. |
| 4 | Chaturthi | **Maa Kushmanda** | Creator of the universe. Holds pot of nectar. Brings health & vitality. Brightest solar form. |
| 5 | Panchami | **Maa Skandamata** | Mother of Kartikeya (Skanda). Grants wisdom, victory & moksha. Four-armed lotus bearer. |
| 6 | Shashthi | **Maa Katyayani** | Born in sage Katyayan's ashram. Destroyer of Mahishasura. Represents courage. |
| 7 | Saptami | **Maa Kalaratri** | Fierce dark form. Destroyer of darkness & enemies. Symbol of fearlessness. Also called Shubhankari. |
| 8 | Ashtami | **Maa Mahagauri** | The extremely fair one. Eight-year-old form. Symbol of purity, peace & calmness. |
| 9 | Navami | **Maa Siddhidatri** | Giver of all supernatural powers (Siddhis). Emerged from Shiva's left half. Worshipped by all gods. |

### Chaitra Special

> The 9th day (Navami) is simultaneously **Ram Navami** — the birth of Lord Rama — making Chaitra Navratri also called Rama Navratri. This convergence of Shakti worship and Dharma embodiment is unique to this season.

## Ashadha Gupt Navratri — Dasha Mahavidya + Nava Varahi

*Secret tradition · Tantric & Shakta path · June–July*

> In Gupt Navratris, each ratri is dedicated to one of the **10 Dasha Mahavidyas** — the tantric cosmic wisdom goddesses. Ashadha is additionally associated with the 9 forms of **Maa Varahi** (Nava Varahi), one each night.

### The 10 Dasha Mahavidyas (core of both Gupt Navratris)

| # | Mahavidya | Description |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Maa Kali** | First & foremost. Goddess of time, destruction & liberation. Dark form, dissolves ego. |
| 2 | **Maa Tara** | Saviour. Saved Shiva from Halahal poison. Fierce compassion, guides through darkness. |
| 3 | **Tripura Sundari (Shodashi)** | Most beautiful in three worlds. 16 types of desires. Wealth, beauty & harmony. |
| 4 | **Maa Bhuvaneshwari** | Creator of all planets & worlds. Rules the cosmos. Grants prosperity against all odds. |
| 5 | **Maa Bhairavi** | Warrior goddess. Consort of Bhairav. Removes ignorance, miseries & sorrows. |
| 6 | **Maa Chinnamasta** | Self-severed head. Giver & taker of life. Self-sacrifice, quenching of all knowledge. |
| 7 | **Maa Dhumavati** | Widow goddess. Depicts sorrow & imperfection. Teaches seeing beyond illusion (maya). |
| 8 | **Maa Bagalamukhi** | Paralyser of enemies. Crane-headed. Grants victory in disputes & legal matters. |
| 9 | **Maa Matangi** | Tantric Saraswati. Goddess of art, speech & inner knowing. Worshipped at dusk. |
| 10 | **Maa Kamala** | Tantric Lakshmi. Goddess of wealth, beauty & prosperity. Seated on lotus. |

### Nava Varahi — 9 Night Forms Unique to Ashadha

| Night | Form | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Swapna Varahi** | Protects from nightmares, psychic attacks & hidden fears. |
| 2 | **Maha Varahi** | Supreme form. Commander of Lalita Devi's army. Grants fearlessness. |
| 3 | **Kroda Varahi** | Anger form. Destroys enemies & negative forces swiftly. |
| 4 | **Vikrita Varahi** | Distorted form. Breaks illusions & removes karmic obstacles. |
| 5 | **Varahi Panchami** | Fifth-day form. Governs senses. Activates Chaitanya Tattva. |
| 6 | **Ajna Varahi** | Third-eye form. Grants clear vision, intuition & spiritual sight. |
| 7 | **Dandini Varahi** | Holds staff (Danda). Shapes thoughts toward successful action. |
| 8 | **Mrita Sanjivani Varahi** | Life-restoring form. Heals diseases & grants longevity. |
| 9 | **Vaishnavi Varahi** | Vaishnava form. Union of Shakti & Vishnu energies. Liberation. |

## Sharad Navratri (Maha Navratri) — 9 Navdurgas

*Ashwin month · Most widely celebrated · Ends in Vijayadashami*

> The same 9 Navdurgas as Chaitra — but rooted in the Mahishasura Mardini legend. Sandhi Puja at the Ashtami–Navami junction marks when Chamunda appeared to slay Chanda & Munda. Generally has three thematic arcs: first three days = Durga (power), next three = Lakshmi (wealth), final three = Saraswati (wisdom).

### The Nine Forms (with Phase Themes)

| Ratri | Tithi | Devi | Phase | Meaning |
|-------|-------|------|-------|---------|
| 1 | Pratipada | **Maa Shailputri** | 🔴 Durga | Daughter of Himalaya. Begins the nine-day spiritual journey. Bull (Nandi) as vehicle. |
| 2 | Dwitiya | **Maa Brahmacharini** | 🔴 Durga | The austere devotee. Carries rosary & kamandalu. Embodies tapasya & willpower. |
| 3 | Tritiya | **Maa Chandraghanta** | 🔴 Durga | Ten-armed warrior. Half-moon bell. Destroys demons, blesses devotees with peace. |
| 4 | Chaturthi | **Maa Kushmanda** | 🟢 Lakshmi | Cosmic creator. Her smile lit the universe. Eight-armed, rides lion. |
| 5 | Panchami | **Maa Skandamata** | 🟢 Lakshmi | Mother energy. Holds infant Kartikeya. Grants mercy & the power of discernment. |
| 6 | Shashthi | **Maa Katyayani** | 🟢 Lakshmi | Warrior avatar. Arose to slay Mahishasura. Four-armed, rides lion, grants courage. |
| 7 | Saptami | **Maa Kalaratri** | 🔵 Saraswati | Dark, fearsome destroyer of demons Shumbha & Nishumbha. Called Shubhankari — the auspicious one. |
| 8 | Ashtami | **Maa Mahagauri** | 🔵 Saraswati | Radiant white goddess. Sandhi Puja falls here. Purifies sins, restores lustre & purity. |
| 9 | Navami | **Maa Siddhidatri** | 🔵 Saraswati | Fulfiller of all Siddhis. Worshipped even by Shiva. Kanya Puja (9 girls) performed today. |

*🔴 Durga phase · 🟢 Lakshmi phase · 🔵 Saraswati phase*

## Magha Gupt Navratri — Dasha Mahavidyas

*Magha month (Jan–Feb) · Winter · Shishir Navratri*

> Like Ashadha, Magha Gupt Navratri is centred on the **10 Dasha Mahavidyas**, with each ratri dedicated to one Mahavidya. The 5th day independently coincides with **Vasant Panchami**, where Maa Saraswati (who is also Mahavidya 9 — Matangi) is widely worshipped. The period is believed to be when the Mahavidyas are most accessible to sincere seekers.

### The 10 Dasha Mahavidyas (Same as Ashadha, Same Sequence)

| Night | Mahavidya | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **Maa Kali** | Invoked on the dark Amavasya before Pratipada. Time consciousness. Destroys illusion. |
| 2 | **Maa Tara** | Fierce compassion. Guides through suffering. Liberates with strength & endurance. |
| 3 | **Tripura Sundari** | Sri Vidya — the highest tantric path. Beauty, bliss & the union of all desire. |
| 4 | **Maa Bhuvaneshwari** | Space goddess. Mother of worlds. Grants abundance where none seems possible. |
| 5 ★ | **Maa Bhairavi / Vasant Panchami** | Day 5 merges with Vasant Panchami — Saraswati worshipped alongside Bhairavi. Arts, music & learning. |
| 6 | **Maa Chinnamasta** | Radical self-sacrifice. Feeds her devotees with her own blood. Transcends duality. |
| 7 | **Maa Dhumavati** | Elder widow. Teaches acceptance of impermanence & the void beyond form. |
| 8 | **Maa Bagalamukhi** | Stunner of speech & movement. Silences enemies. Grants power of command. |
| 9 | **Maa Matangi** | Tantric Saraswati. Inner music, sacred speech & art. Worshipped at twilight. |
| 10 | **Maa Kamala** | Tantric Lakshmi. Tenth & final wisdom. Lotus-seated grace, abundance & liberation. |

> Note: While there are 10 Mahavidyas and 9 ratris, traditions vary on how the days are mapped. Some sources combine Kali's invocation with the pre-Navratri Amavasya; others pair two Mahavidyas on certain days. The Dashami (10th) carries its own puja mirroring Vijayadashami's significance.
