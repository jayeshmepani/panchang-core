## 📅 Part 2: The Full 26-Ekadashi Cycle (2026)
*Includes the rare intercalary Adhik Maas dates.*

### 1. Shattila Ekadashi — 14 Jan 2026 (Wed)
*   **Significance:** Focuses on *Tila* (sesame) rituals. Includes sesame baths and donations to cure poverty and purify sins.

### 2. Jaya Ekadashi — 29 Jan 2026 (Thu)
*   **Significance:** Liberates souls from the lower ghostly realms (*Pisach Yoni*). 

### 3. Vijaya Ekadashi — 13 Feb 2026 (Fri)
*   **Significance:** "Victory Ekadashi." Lord Rama observed this before crossing the sea to Lanka on Sage Bakadalbhya’s advice.

### 4. Amalaki Ekadashi — 27 Feb 2026 (Fri)
*   **Significance:** Dedicated to the Amla tree (Gooseberry). Vishnu and Lakshmi are believed to reside in the tree on this day.

### 5. Papmochani Ekadashi — 15 Mar 2026 (Sun)
*   **Significance:** "Liberator from Sin." The final Ekadashi before the Hindu New Year (Chaitra).

### 6. Kamada Ekadashi — 29 Mar 2026 (Sun)
*   **Significance:** "Wish-fulfilling." The first Ekadashi of the standard Vikram Samvat 2083.

### 7. Varuthini Ekadashi — 13 Apr 2026 (Mon)
*   **Significance:** Provides a protective "armor" (*Varut*) for the soul. Merit is equal to donating ten thousand cows.

### 8. Mohini Ekadashi — 27 Apr 2026 (Mon)
*   **Significance:** Commemorates Vishnu’s Mohini Avatar. Helps the devotee detach from worldly illusions (Maya).

### 9. Apara Ekadashi — 13 May 2026 (Wed)
*   **Significance:** Grants "Boundless" (*Apara*) merit. Particularly effective against sins of false witness or lying.

### ⭐ 10. Padmini (Kamala) Ekadashi — 27 May 2026 (Wed)
*   **Rare Adhik Maas:** Occurs in the Shukla Paksha of the extra month. Blesses couples with long-awaited progeny.

### ⭐ 11. Parama Ekadashi — 11 June 2026 (Thu)
*   **Rare Adhik Maas:** Occurs in the Krishna Paksha of the extra month. Known to destroy even the most deep-seated poverty.

### 12. Nirjala Ekadashi — 25 June 2026 (Thu)
*   **The King of Ekadashis:** The most austere fast of the year. **No food or water** is consumed from sunrise to sunrise. 

### 13. Yogini Ekadashi — 11 July 2026 (Sat)
*   **Significance:** Specifically known for curing chronic skin diseases. Story of the gardener Hemamali.

### 14. Devshayani (Ashadhi) Ekadashi — 25 July 2026 (Sat)
*   **Chaturmas Begins:** Vishnu enters Yoga Nidra (cosmic sleep). Auspicious ceremonies (weddings) are suspended for 4 months.

### 15. Kamika Ekadashi — 09 Aug 2026 (Sun)
*   **Significance:** Worshiping with Tulsi leaves today removes the sin of *Brahma-hatya* and frees the soul from Yamaraj's judgment.

### 16. Putrada / Pavitropana — 24 Aug 2026 (Mon)
*   **Significance:** Grants children. In Gujarat, it is "Pavitropana," a day to purify one's religious duties and vows.

### 17. Aja Ekadashi — 07 Sep 2026 (Mon)
*   **Significance:** Story of King Harishchandra. Fasting on this day restores lost status and destroys 7 lifetimes of accumulated sin.

### 18. Parivartini (Vamana) — 22 Sep 2026 (Tue)
*   **Significance:** Lord Vishnu "turns over" from his left side to his right in his sleep. Associated with the Vamana Avatar.

### 19. Indira Ekadashi — 06 Oct 2026 (Tue)
*   **Pitru Paksha:** Falls in the fortnight of ancestors. Observing this can liberate a sinful ancestor from hell.

### 20. Papankusha Ekadashi — 22 Oct 2026 (Thu)
*   **Significance:** "Goad against sin." A powerful day to destroy the ego and accumulated negative karma.

### 21. Rama Ekadashi — 05 Nov 2026 (Thu)
*   **Significance:** Occurs 3 days before Diwali. Dedicated to Lakshmi (Rama) to invite abundance before the festival of lights.

### 22. Devutthana (Prabodhini) — 21 Nov 2026 (Sat)
*   **Chaturmas Ends:** Vishnu wakes. **Tulsi Vivah** is performed, marking the start of the Hindu wedding season.

### 23. Utpanna Ekadashi — 04 Dec 2026 (Fri)
*   **Significance:** Celebrating the manifestation of Ekadashi Devi from Vishnu's body to slay the demon Mura.

### 24. Mokshada Ekadashi — 20 Dec 2026 (Sun)
*   **Significance:** Gita Jayanti. Grants liberation (*Moksha*). Vaikuntha Dwaram is opened in many temples.

### 📌 Summary of Key Rules
1.  **Smartha vs. Vaishnava:** The dates above follow the **Smartha** (sunrise) system. Vaishnava/ISKCON followers sometimes fast a day later if the Ekadashi tithi is overlapping with the previous day at dawn.
2.  **Parana (Fast Break):** Fasts must be broken the following day (*Dwadashi*) after sunrise.
3.  **Adhik Maas (⭐):** The rare extra month in 2026 adds two powerful Ekadashis specifically dedicated to **Lord Purushottam** (Vishnu).
4.  **Month Differences:** In the Krishna Paksha, the month name used in the North (UP/Bihar/Delhi) is usually one name ahead of the month name used in the West (Gujarat/Maharashtra). The date of the fast remains identical.
